Sequence Trip Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026

Sequence Trip (package dev.stimmie.oeisvisualizations) is an app for exploring and animating integer sequences from the On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences (OEIS). This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your data.

Data we collect

None. Sequence Trip has no user accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. We cannot identify you.

How the app works

The full sequence dataset ships inside the app as an on-device database. Search and browsing run entirely offline on your device. Nothing about your activity leaves your device for our servers, because we do not operate any.

Network requests

The app makes two kinds of network requests, neither of which identifies you:

OEIS fetches. When you open a sequence that needs additional terms (b-files) or the full entry, the app fetches them from the public OEIS website (oeis.org). These requests contain the sequence identifier you are viewing and standard technical information (such as your IP address, which any web request necessarily includes). They are handled by OEIS under their own terms; we neither see nor log them.

App update checks. On launch, the app contacts Expo's update service (EAS Update) to check for and download bug fixes to the app's JavaScript code that we have published. These requests include the app's version, runtime, and platform; they contain no user identifiers, no account information, and nothing about your activity in the app. Expo's handling of these requests is described in the Expo privacy policy.

Permissions

The app requests internet access, used solely for the requests described above. If you turn on the daily sequence reminder, it also asks for permission to show notifications; reminders are scheduled entirely on your device and send nothing to anyone.

Children

The app collects no data from anyone, including children.
